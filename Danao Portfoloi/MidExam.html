Chinese Zodiac Signs · 生肖

The 12 Animals · 60‑Year Cycle

The Chinese Zodiac is a sophisticated system that blends the 12 animal signs with the Five Elements to create a unique 60‑year cycle. Your personality is shaped not only by the animal of your birth year but also by whether that year was influenced by Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, or Water. For example, a "Water Tiger" is seen as more sensitive and intuitive than the assertive and ambitious "Metal Tiger." This complex interplay ensures that even people born under the same animal sign can have vastly different temperaments and life paths.

Beyond individual traits, the Shengxiao serves as a vital cultural guide for determining interpersonal compatibility and timing major life events. Many people consult the zodiac to find ideal partners, as certain animal pairings like the Pig and the Tiger are believed to bring natural harmony and prosperity. Additionally, specific years are viewed as more auspicious for starting businesses or having children, deeply influencing social trends across generations. This ancient tradition remains a powerful tool for understanding one's place within the natural and social world.

History & Origins · 历史

The Chinese zodiac, known as Shēngxiào (生肖), formally developed during the Han Dynasty (206 BCE–220 CE), though its roots trace back to the Warring States period (475–221 BCE). Archaeological discoveries at Yunmeng Shuihudi and Tianshui Fangmatan (c. 300–220 BCE) contain the earliest complete lists of zodiac animals, confirming the system existed before imperial unification. The custom of pairing animals with years can be traced back to at least the Han dynasty, with many legends and folktales surrounding the 12 zodiac animals depicted in East Asian art.

According to legend, the Jade Emperor invited all animals to a race across a river. The order of arrival determined their place in the cycle: the clever Rat hitched a ride on the Ox, jumping ahead at the last moment; the Dragon delayed to bring rain to villages; the Dog stopped to bathe; and the Pig paused to eat. The Cat was tricked by the Rat and missed the race entirely—explaining why cats are absent from the zodiac. An alternative theory suggests the order was based on animals' toes/hooves, alternating between even and odd numbers—the Rat was first because it has four toes on front legs and five on rear, combining both even and odd.

The twelve animals correspond to the Earthly Branches (地支), a duodecimal system used for timekeeping since the Shang Dynasty (1600–1046 BCE). Ancient Chinese scholars matched these branches with animals to make them easier to remember, creating the symbolic system still used today across East Asia. The use of animals to mark years spread throughout East and Central Asia, with local adaptations—Vietnam uses a water buffalo instead of ox and cat instead of rabbit; Turkic peoples replaced tiger with leopard, pig with elephant, and rat with camel.

The Five Elements & Their Traits

History & Philosophy · 五行

The theory of the Five Elements, known as Wu Xing (五行) in Chinese, originated during the Warring States period (475–221 BCE) and was fully developed by the Han Dynasty (206 BCE–220 CE). Unlike the Greek four-element system, Wu Xing describes five dynamic "phases" or "movements"—Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, and Water—that constantly interact, transform, and influence all aspects of life. This philosophy became fundamental to traditional Chinese culture, extending far beyond astrology into medicine, music, military strategy, feng shui, and martial arts. The five elements are associated with everything from seasons and directions to colors, organs, and emotions.

The Cycle of Balance

The elements follow two complementary cycles that maintain harmony in the universe :

Generative Cycle (Sheng): Wood feeds Fire → Fire creates Earth (ash) → Earth bears Metal → Metal carries Water → Water nourishes Wood

Controlling Cycle (Ke): Wood breaks Earth → Earth absorbs Water → Water quenches Fire → Fire melts Metal → Metal chops Wood

The Five Elements

Your specific element is determined by the last digit of your birth year. This element combines with your zodiac animal to create the 60-year cycle.

  • Metal
    (years ending in 0 or 1)     Associated with autumn, white, and the planet Venus. Represents strength, focus, and determination. These "driven organizers" are disciplined, goal-oriented, and value structure—but can become rigid or judgmental. Metal finds balance through Water and is tempered by Fire.
  • Water
    (years ending in 2 or 3)     Associated with winter, black, and the planet Mercury. Symbolizes wisdom, intuition, and adaptability. Water types are introspective, empathetic communicators who "go with the flow"—yet may isolate themselves when overwhelmed. Water is nourished by Metal and controlled by Earth.
  • Wood
    (years ending in 4 or 5)     Associated with spring, green, and the planet Jupiter. Represents growth, creativity, and generosity. These "grounded visionaries" value community, plan thoughtfully, and foster growth—but can become overly competitive. Wood feeds Fire and is shaped by Metal.
  • Fire
    (years ending in 6 or 7)     Associated with summer, red, and the planet Mars. Represents passion, energy, and leadership. Fire types are charismatic thrill-seekers who inspire others—but risk burning out or becoming impulsive. Fire creates Earth and is controlled by Water.
  • Earth
    (years ending in 8 or 9)     Associated with late summer, yellow/brown, and the planet Saturn. Represents stability, reliability, and practicality. These "thoughtful mediators" provide balance, nurture others, and remain calm in crises—but may become controlling when unappreciated. Earth produces Metal and is nourished by Fire.
